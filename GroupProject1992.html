Overview

In 1992 the Olympic Games were held twice in one year: the Winter Games in Albertville, France and the Summer Games in Barcelona, Spain. It was the last time that Winter and Summer Olympics were in the same year.

  • Winter Games: 8–23 February 1992 (Albertville)
  • Summer Games: 25 July – 9 August 1992 (Barcelona)
  • Unified Team (former Soviet republics) competed together
  • No boycotts at the Summer Games
  • NBA players were allowed, creating the famous USA “Dream Team”

Barcelona 1992 – Summer Olympics

Barcelona used the Games to modernise the city. The waterfront and harbour were rebuilt, new sports venues were created and transport was improved. After 1992 the city became a major tourist destination.

Basic Facts

  • Host city: Barcelona, Spain
  • Nations: more than 165
  • Athletes: over 9,000
  • Sports: 25

Highlights

  • Archer Antonio Rebollo lit the cauldron with a flaming arrow.
  • The USA “Dream Team” dominated men’s basketball.
  • Linford Christie won the men’s 100m race.
  • Derartu Tulu became the first Black African woman to win Olympic gold.
  • Swimmers Alexander Popov and Krisztina Egerszegi won several medals.

Albertville 1992 – Winter Olympics

The Winter Games were held in the French Alps around the town of Albertville. Many events took place in ski resorts in the mountains near the town.

Basic Facts

  • Host city: Albertville, France
  • Nations: more than 60
  • Athletes: about 1,800
  • Sports: 12

Important Events

  • Short-track speed skating added as a medal sport.
  • Moguls became an official freestyle skiing event.
  • Figure skating and alpine skiing were very popular with viewers.

Legacy of the 1992 Olympics

  • Barcelona’s waterfront and city layout were greatly improved.
  • Tourism increased strongly after the Games.
  • 1992 was the last year with Winter and Summer Games in the same year.
  • The Games helped start a more modern and commercial Olympic era.
